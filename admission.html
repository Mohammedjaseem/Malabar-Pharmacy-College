Admission

Malabar College of Pharmacy, approved by the Pharmacy Council of India and affiliated to Kerala University of Health Sciences, offers a Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm) with a duration of 4 years.

B.Pharm Course

Selection of candidates for admission to B.Pharm course in Malabar College of Pharmacy will be purely on a merit basis. Details pertaining to admission are as follows.

Details of Seats

Total Number of Seats: 60, with 30 seats open to all candidates under General Merit and 30 seats available through Management Quota. All applicants are eligible to be considered under General Merit.

Eligibility for Admission

Citizenship: Only Indian nationals are eligible to apply.
Academic Qualification: Applicants must have successfully completed the Higher Secondary Examination in Kerala or an equivalent examination, securing a minimum of 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics.
Age: Candidates must be at least 17 years old as of December 31, 2025. There is no upper age limit for the B.Pharm program, although this may be revised by PCI/KUHS in the future.

Procedure of Admission:

Selected Candidates shall take admission at the appointed time and date in the College after paying the Tuition fee, Caution Deposit, and Special fee.

Documents to be submitted at the time of Admission:
  • → 10th Certificate
  • → Plus Two Certificate
  • → Transfer Certificate
  • → Conduct Certificate
  • → Migration Certificate
  • → Eligibility Certificate from KUHS if applicable
  • → Copy of AADHAR card
  • → Passport Size Photo
  • → Physical Fitness certificate from Medical officer
Online Application Requirements

The online application should contain the following particulars:

  1. 1) College Details: Name of the Pharmacy College with Address and Registration details should be included in the online application form.
  2. 2) Auto-generated Tracking: The online application form should contain Auto-generated Registration/Application Number and auto-generate date and time of submission of application.
  3. 3) Upload Facility: The facility for uploading the photograph and signature of the candidate shall be provided.
  4. 4) Quota Specification: The category/quota shall be clearly specified in the online application form.
  5. 5) Candidate Declaration: A declaration by the candidate that the details provided are true to the best of their knowledge must be included.
  6. 6) Print Facility: The facility for taking a printout of the application shall be provided to the candidate by the Pharmacy College.
